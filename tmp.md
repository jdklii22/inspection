Overall Meeting Objective:\
To review the status of a pending bank acquisition, address regulatory approval timing uncertainties, and discuss related operational considerations, recent wholesale loan purchase activity, and upcoming debt financing plans.

* * * * *

Key Discussion Points:

1.  Acquisition Closing Timeline and Operational Constraints\
    The team analyzed the critical deadline for receiving regulatory approval by February 13th to facilitate a March 1st closing. The discussion explained the mandatory 15-day DOJ cooling-off period post-approval and the operational risks of mid-month closings. It was noted that 90% of bank acquisitions close on the first of the month because mid-period closings create significant accounting challenges, including difficulty obtaining fully reconciled balance sheets due to intra-month payment activity. Missing the February 13th deadline would necessitate pushing the closing to April 1st, which would also allow more time for post-close purchase accounting activities before the March 31st reporting deadline.

2.  Regulatory Approval Status and Uncertainty\
    The last correspondence from regulators (Philly Fed) was received on the 13th, but no clear timeline or deadline has been provided by Washington officials. The lack of visibility into the approval process is creating organizational anxiety. Participants expressed concern about the uncertainty and discussed the need for proactive outreach to obtain a more definitive timeline.

3.  Wholesale Loan Portfolio Purchase\
    The team successfully closed a $207 million loan purchase transaction the previous night at an attractive discount. The portfolio has been assigned to account managers, and a strategy is being implemented for direct customer outreach to solicit deposits and deepen commercial relationships, which is expected to support balance sheet growth throughout the year.

4.  Balance Sheet Optimization Strategy\
    There is active consideration of selling underperforming, higher-risk loans ("razzy paper") to improve collective loan yields and capital ratios. This would be contingent on favorable market conditions and pricing. The organization's capital position is performing better than originally projected in the acquisition pro forma, creating flexibility for such a "wash trade"---selling risk-weighted loans and potentially replacing them with lower-risk securities or commercial paper.

5.  Debt Refinancing and Capital Planning\
    Plans are being developed for a $300 million subordinated note offering in late April/early May. The structure would refinance $200 million of existing debt while adding $100 million in new capital. The additional $100 million would generate approximately $5.5 million in pre-tax earnings and provide flexibility for potential future acquisitions. The timing is contingent upon first-quarter earnings results and board approval, with market conditions currently appearing receptive.

Highlights:

-   Successfully closed a $207 million wholesale loan purchase at a favorable discount
-   Capital position is exceeding original acquisition pro forma projections
-   Market conditions are favorable for executing a $300 million debt refinancing
-   All operational and legal preparations for the acquisition are complete pending regulatory approval
-   The organization is proactively pursuing multiple balance sheet optimization strategies
